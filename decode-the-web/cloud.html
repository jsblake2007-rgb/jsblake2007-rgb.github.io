01 — Cloud Computing, Defined

Someone else's computer. Everyone's superpower.

Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services — servers, storage, databases, networking, software, analytics, and intelligence — over the internet ("the cloud").

Instead of buying hardware and running your own datacenter, you rent exactly what you need from a provider's global network and pay only for what you use. The result: faster innovation, flexible resources, and economies of scale that no single server room can match.

  • Lower operating costs — no capital spend on hardware
  • Elastic scale — resources grow and shrink with demand
  • Faster shipping — provision infrastructure in minutes, not months
cloud-shell: ~/deploy 
# the old way
$ buy servers; wait 8 weeks; build datacenter
# the cloud way
$ cloud create --servers 200 --region global
✓ provisioned in 94 seconds
$ cloud scale --down --pay-for none
✓ you only pay for what runs▋

02 — Why It Won

Seven reasons the world moved to the cloud

A big shift from the traditional way businesses think about IT — and the reasons keep compounding.

💸

Cost

No buying hardware, no powering server racks, no 24/7 cooling bill. Capital expense becomes a pay-as-you-go line item.

⚡

Speed

Vast computing resources provisioned in minutes, self-service, with a few clicks — capacity planning stops being a bottleneck.

🌍

Global scale

Scale elastically: the right amount of compute, storage, and bandwidth, exactly when needed, from the right geographic location.

🎯

Productivity

No racking, stacking, or patching. IT teams spend their hours on goals that move the business, not maintenance chores.

🚀

Performance

A worldwide network of secure datacenters on the latest-generation hardware — lower latency than any single corporate server room.

🛟

Reliability

Backup, disaster recovery, and business continuity get cheaper and easier when data is mirrored across redundant sites.

🛡️

Security

Providers ship a deep set of policies, technologies, and controls that harden your posture — protecting data, apps, and infrastructure against threats most teams couldn't counter alone.

03 — Deployment Models

Not all clouds are the same cloud

Before you pick services, you pick an architecture. There are three ways to deploy.

Public cloud

Owned and operated by a third-party provider that delivers servers and storage over the internet. You manage your account from a browser; they manage everything else.

AzureAWSGoogle Cloud

Private cloud

Cloud resources used exclusively by one organization — hosted in its own datacenter or by a third party, but always on a private network. Maximum control and isolation.

ExclusiveOn-premPrivate network

Hybrid cloud

Public and private, bound together so data and apps move between them. Greater flexibility, more deployment options, and a path that respects existing security and compliance.

Public+Private

04 — The Cloud Stack

Four layers, one stack

Cloud services build on top of one another — that's why it's called a stack. The higher you go, the more the provider manages for you.

SaaS

Software as a Service

Finished applications delivered over the internet, usually by subscription. The provider hosts the app, the infrastructure, the upgrades, and the security patching — you just open a browser and work.

you manage: almost nothing · provider: everything

FaaS

Serverless computing

Build app functionality without touching the servers that run it. Event-driven and highly scalable — resources spin up only when a function or trigger fires, and you pay for nothing while idle.

you manage: code & events · provider: servers, scaling, capacity

PaaS

Platform as a Service

An on-demand environment for developing, testing, delivering, and managing apps. Build web and mobile products fast without configuring the servers, storage, network, or databases underneath.

you manage: apps & data · provider: runtime, OS, infrastructure

IaaS

Infrastructure as a Service

The foundation. Rent servers, virtual machines, storage, networks, and operating systems pay-as-you-go — maximum control over your environment without owning a single machine.

you manage: OS & up · provider: hardware, network, datacenter
